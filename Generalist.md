> We all have extraordinary creative, humanitarian, and spiritual possibilities but are often alienated from them because we are so focused on a very narrow slice of who we are.

Diversifying our identity allows for survival through change.

This is an important value in Western society, where we can unhealthily connect our identity with our job. If we lose our job, we should have other things to find meaning in. We are adaptable.

We are less prone to [[Burnout]] from hyper-fixated productivity, since we care about our other identities and interests.

Being a [[Polymath]] is a more extreme version of a generalist, since not only are you fascinated by a wide range of things, you are also skilled at them.

We can cultivate our set of interests through [[Alignment]]

A common route for people with [[ADHD]].
