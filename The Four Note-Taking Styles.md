## What is a Second Brain

A Second Brain is a system for managing knowledge. It is a trusted place outside your head to store your most important ideas, resources, and insights. The core of a Second Brain is a digital notes app. It serves as long-term memory for capturing and organizing information.

The right app aligns with your natural thinking style. Using a tool that does not match your style can lead to frustration, while using one that fits can unlock energy and creativity.

## Overview of the Styles

There are four main note-taking styles: Architect, Gardener, Librarian, and Student. Each style reflects a different way of interacting with information and building a Second Brain.

### Architect

- > Architects want to fit all their information into an all-encompassing "ultimate system" with a clear hierarchy. They prefer [[Top-Down Thinking]], designing their notes and structure from the general framework down to the details.

### Gardener

- > Gardeners are the exact opposite of Architects. They tend to think in a [[Bottom-Up Thinking]] way, most at home imagining, dreaming, wandering, and making spontaneous connections in a way that no upfront plan could ever predict.

### Librarian

- > Librarians have a fundamentally practical relationship to information. They value books and ideas for their own sake but also seek to organize information for specific purposes. They have a deep desire to find and save the most useful, interesting knowledge.

### Student

- > Student types are often beginning their note-taking journey. They are merely novices in knowledge management. The scope and complexity of their notes are limited to specific use cases. This is the most common type.
